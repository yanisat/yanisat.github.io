The Boston Anti-Highway Movement

In 1948, the State of Massachusetts had plans for eight highways through and around the Greater Boston Area, threatening to rip apart communities and alter the landscape of the area for the worse. By 1972, seven of the plans had been canceled. Between 1950's and the 1980's, a broad coalition of neighborhood residents, activists, urban planners, academics, and government officials came together to protest these highways and to create new functions for the areas left behind.
